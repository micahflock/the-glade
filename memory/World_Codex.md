# World Codex
# The Glade — World Lore, Zones, Factions, and Locations
# Narrative reference file. Update via /scribe only.

---

## The World

A dark fantasy world inhabited entirely by insects and other arthropods. Scale accordingly — a mouse pelt is a trophy, a pillbug can pull a cart, and a scorpion's stinger is a museum piece. Human-scale objects are monuments. Weather is catastrophic. No horizon is visible in the glade — the massive trunks of trees create a staggered blind in every direction. The world feels grounded and slightly weary, not high-fantasy heroic.

**Scale reference:** It takes roughly 5 minutes or less to walk around a large tree trunk. Pillbugs and sowbugs are draft animals. Millipedes, saddlebags bulging, carry entire families. Isopods are mounts.

**Tone:** Miyazaki whimsy to Del Toro horror. Ordinary hardship alongside genuine wonder. Nobody is simply evil — motivations are grounded in survival. The horror of the Bloom is mostly quiet: empty fields, scentless forest, doors wrenched off hinges. It announces itself in absences.

## The Bloom

An unnatural winter arrived without warning mid-summer — felt first as a gust of wind from the depths of winter cutting through a balmy midsummer day. Travelers trickled in with tales of snowfall smothering new summer growth and wild raiders devastating towns. That trickle grew to a stream, then a flood of refugees from the east.

The Royal Jelly Society suspects the cold is expanding outward from a single epicenter to the east — not a great wind, but a shockwave of cold expanding from a central point. Dr. T'wigfingers cross-referenced arrival dates of messengers, lost-contact records with various towns, and timing of refugee asylum requests. It all points to one thing: an epicenter.

Most of the population doesn't know how bad it is. The authorities are managing information carefully to prevent panic. Alongside the cold, Garett's instruments in the Antennington Amber Mine detected a slight fluctuation in the magical flow — a magical shockwave that appears linked to the cold. Garett coined the term "The Bloom."

Spring has not come. Weeks late at this point.

## The Big Bad — Origin Event (DM Only)

The central ant queen (fall zone) was attacked by a large cordyceps-infected spider. Bitten by the spider and desperate to save her colony, she used forbidden ant coven magic to trap the spider in a stasis bubble and slow her own infection.

### Side Effects

**The seasonal freeze.** The freezing spell caused an annular seasonal freeze radiating outward from the epicenter. The world is now concentric rings of seasons frozen in time.

**Coven corruption.** Breaking the coven's pact against this dark magic branch has left all ant queens in the region more powerful and slightly more corrupted. This is why they've gone mad and are raiding (also survival). Ant coven magic binds the ant queens together.

**Cordyceps control.** The queen herself remains partially infected and can now control anything infected with "her" strain of cordyceps. 

### Open Questions

- Was the queen attacked unprovoked, or did she bring it on herself (evil research)? Current lean: attacked by the spider.
- Does the Bloom affect all magic users, or just ant queens? If all magic users: termite madness in summer zone, explanation for PC rapid leveling, more interesting monsters. Both DMs lean toward all magic users.
- The spider will have to be dealt with to unfreeze everything.
- The ant queen is a cordyceps lich now, but may be convinced to undo her magic — which will cause her to turn on the party at some point.

### Earlier Concept Notes

Original concept: a force (evil spirit, enchanted item) that freezes time, with the caster unwilling or unable to undo it because the original disaster would then occur. Alternative framings explored: a pupa that doesn't want to grow up; a mad wizard protecting an infant from a falling bookcase; a vain artificer dropping a fancy creation. All led to the current "desperate queen freezing infection" version.

## Zone Structure

The world radiates outward from the epicenter in concentric seasonal rings. It has not been like this for generations — roughly a decade at least in the inner zones, with winter expanding more recently.

| Zone    | Climate                          | Main Threats                              | Travel Width  |
|---------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|---------------|
| Winter  | Permanent frost, no decay, no scent | Earwig bandits, desperation, red ant raids | Current party location |
| Spring  | Boundless growth, nothing rots   | Fire ant + leafcutter alliance, flash floods, big raindrops | ~3 days |
| Summer  | Blasted heat, dryness, fire      | Tentatively termites, cordyceps zombies   | ~2 days       |
| Fall    | Rot, slime, mushroom, wet decay  | Cordyceps ant queen, zombie bugs          | ~1+ day to center |

**The Rise.** The wavefront/barrier between seasonal zones. Challenging to traverse. One concept: the winter/spring barrier is 20 feet of gelatinous cube with a very sharp delineation — the other side is clearly spring. Physics may be otherwise weird. Underdeveloped.

### Winter Zone (Current Party Location)

Permanent frost, no decay, no scent. Villages burnt or ransacked. Farms frozen mid-growth — stalks and husks locked upright in the frozen earth, grey and desiccated, neither rotting nor living. The ground has been frozen long enough that even the gentle sweet decay of leaf litter is absent.

### Spring Zone

Nothing rots, things only grow. Thick and fresh — no dirt, just paths through soggy smashed young stalks. Twisting layers of green growth. Bee hives overflowing with honey. Stuff grown here magically cannot break down or rot, and this property travels with objects removed from the zone. At least one town to resupply and buy armor. Hazards: flash floods, big raindrops, the ant alliance. Some bugs have adapted and fiercely defend it.

### Summer Zone

Tentatively a blasted wasteland of heat, dryness, and fire. Termites as primary faction and obstacle. Cordyceps zombies start appearing. Content largely unplanned — needs a major challenge or set piece.

### Fall Zone

Rot, slime, mushroom, fungus, moss, wet decay. High-level endgame environment. The cordyceps ant queen is here. The spider must be dealt with to resolve the Bloom.

### Travel Distances (Working Draft)

- Chitinholm → Antennington: ~2 days
- Antennington → Spring zone border: ~3 days
- Spring zone width: ~3 days
- Summer zone width: ~2 days
- Fall zone: at least 1 full day to reach the center
- Open question: are the annular rings the same width or variable?

## The Ant Alliance (Spring Zone)

### Fire Ants

Organized, aggressive, operating with apparent purpose. Associated with fire magic — heated bodies, fire spitting. The leafcutters note that ever since the Bloom, the fire ants have become more magical: whatever small magic makes bugs work normally has been amplified. They do not kill on raids — they take. Bodies are dragged northeast. The fire ant queen is the aggressor in the alliance.

### Leafcutter Ants

Historically peaceful fungus farmers. The Bloom destroyed their food cycle: nothing rots in the spring zone, so organic material doesn't break down for fungus cultivation. They can no longer grow their fungus. The fire ants decided that corpses would be the best substitute. Their queen was coerced into the alliance. Individual workers resent the situation and may be potential allies — "I was born to carry leaves, not drag corpses."

### Alliance Timeline

1. **Antennington raid (first).** Leafcutter ants only, no fire ants. Town ransacked but not burned. Minor building damage — broken windows, knocked-down doors. No bodies. Ineffective and costly.
2. **Alliance formed.** The failure pushed the leafcutter queen to ally with the fire ant queen, who then effectively took control.
3. **Subsequent raids (burnt village, others).** Fire ants joined. Fire magic used. Villages burned. Bodies taken.
4. **Current state.** Fire ants command; leafcutter ants carry out grunt work under duress. When fire ant commanders are killed, leafcutters stand down and want to talk.

### The Fungal Farms

Underground chambers at the spring zone border where ants decompose organic material — including bodies — to grow fungus and feed the colony. May also function as a prison: surviving villagers may still be alive inside as labor or organic resource. Hook: the leafcutter ants who surrender after combat can offer to take the party to the farms.

### Ant Intelligence

Individual ant minions lean toward pretty dumb — consistent with Sven being an unusually eloquent drone. Smarter ants (commanders, queens, specialists) exist as exceptions.

Open idea: the Bloom's magical shockwave may have "awakened" some individual ants, making them smarter and more self-aware. Possible mechanism: a rogue character producing magical ichor and feeding it to select ants. The awakened leafcutter ants the party meets could be part of a nascent resistance. Big swing: if PCs consume this ichor, they gain a level.

### Queen Structure

- **Spring zone:** Leafcutter queen (coerced) + fire ant queen (aggressor). These two are the spring zone bosses.
- **Summer zone:** TBD. Tentatively termites.
- **Fall zone:** The cordyceps ant queen (the big bad).

## Locations

### Chitinholm

A prosperous city built around the base of a massive fir tree. Proud of its wealth and its tradition of sharing it. Currently straining under a doubled population of refugees.

**RJS Branch.** Tucked into the center of Chitinholm. A labyrinthine building of narrow stairwells and crowded shelves. Contains: the meeting room (large table squeezed up the narrow stairwell, overstuffed chairs pushed against walls, messy map tacked to the table), a well-stocked supply room, and stables out back (small paddock, a few stalls, a well, tack room).

**Residential quarter.** Low acorn roofs, stone-and-spittle construction. Many homes have small walled gardens.

**Market square.** Built around an ornately fuming chimney powering the baker and the bugsmith's forge. Packed — Chitinholm has doubled its population. Small stalls hawk anything and everything. The comforting aroma of fresh nectar cakes mingles with the sweet scent of heated iron. Notable vendors: Rhonda (Goliath beetle, bugsmith), Hickum (carpenter bee, baker — nectar cakes, pollen loaves, honey buns), Bigsby (trinket seller, "Bigsby's Buys!").

**The gates.** Large branches arranged in a beautiful cathedral arch, with spider silk expertly spun between the supports. On dewy mornings the dew it catches reflects the sun like hundreds of little windows. Guards: Julien and Micah (named but undeveloped).

**The road out.** Packed with throngs of displaced bugs from every stratum of society. Weevils with polished carapaces rub abdomens with sawflies. Sowbugs, wood louses, and pillbugs pull carts, some in teams, all heavily laden. You descend the root flare of the massive fir. Glancing back, the jagged spire of the RJS is just visible over the crowd. At the forest floor: the convergence of several wide roads cut through the frozen leaf litter.

### The Road East

The road meanders between the mammoth trunks of the glade. Once away from the city and the crowds thin, the forest is nearly scentless — the ground has been frozen long enough that even the gentle sweet decay of leaf litter is absent. The bustling road slowly dwindles to a rutted country path — two carts could not pass each other, though the whole party can walk abreast. Occasionally have to pick around fallen leaves frozen into the path.

Farms appear along the roadside: fields waiting nine months for a thaw that hasn't come. Whatever was harvested is gone. Whatever wasn't stands where it died — stalks and husks locked upright in the frozen earth, grey and desiccated, neither rotting nor living.

### The Burnt Village

A humble settlement on the road to Antennington, built the way small places are — everything oriented toward travelers, toward commerce, toward the hope of both. Fir-needle roofing with acorn cap shingles.

Now: some homes are just foundations, chunks scattered and scorched. Some are half a blackened wall leaning against a collapsed roof. Others simply have a rough crater in place of their door. The first outbuilding looks intact from a distance — then you see the doors are gone. Not broken, not rotted. Wrenched off. Hinges bent outward.

No food. No bodies. Only soot, broken furniture, and random knick-knacks and keepsakes.

Before you see anything, you smell it. Something burnt — not just woodsmoke. Something sharper, heavier, the kind of smell that stays in your antennae long after the source is gone. (DC 12: the smell of a burned village. DC 16: a distinct tang of magical energy.)

Lars was found here, crouched against the last standing wall. DC 8 passive Perception to hear his low, broken chittering from the south side of the square.

### Antennington

To call it a town was always generous. An outpost was closer to the truth, its status owed entirely to the now-faded Antennington Amber Mine. In generations past, much wealth and magical energy was pulled from the earth beneath the town. The riches did not stay with the inhabitants.

A few clustered mycelium brick homes, a town hall, sparse market stalls focused on amber sales, and a small RJS building. Recently ransacked by leafcutter ants. Deadly quiet. Doors broken down or thrown wide open, cabinets emptied, drawers pulled out.

**Antennington RJS Building.** One floor. Small entry area, adjoining hallway, three rooms off the hallway. All ransacked. Ornate wooden door wide open, held by one remaining hinge.

- **Room 1 — Meeting Room.** Mostly intact. Center round table, chairs. One cabinet opened, contents dumped on table. Mostly documents about the mine and past expeditions.
- **Room 2 — Lab.** A mess. Cabinets and boxes of delicate instruments turned out and strewn about. Perception/History DC 12: instruments are mostly for determining the value of stuff from the mine. Note pinned to the wall with a small hand-pickaxe, written in green ink: "ALL RJS MEMBERS MEET IN THE MINES FOR EMERGENCY MEETING -G". Investigation DC 14: find the Tune Hammer.
- **Room 3 — Breakroom / Staff Only.** The most picked-over room. Whatever the ransackers were looking for, they found the most of it here. Slightly neater than the others — they had to clean up after themselves as they made the mess.

---

### The Antennington Amber Mine (AAM)

Once the pride and joy of the town. Pulling amber out of the ground brought much commerce and travel. As it got harder and demand lessened, the mine became less lucrative. Now mostly maintained as a remnant of history — a motion the town keeps going through because it doesn't know what else to do.

A relatively shallow pit mine. The main building is easy to find — follow the signs. Front door with a pickaxe-shaped knocker. Interior is one room: Ricko works at a desk on a piece of amber.

The hoist/lift: Ricko opens the horizontal gate, then the vertical gate. The lift rumbles menacingly but safely down to Level 2. On the landing: a mildly lit table where Garett works, fussily hunched over instruments.

The mine has two known occupants and has not been touched by the ants. Mining on Level 2 stopped years ago. Garett needed the isolation for his instruments to get their best measurements. Antennington was hit approximately 1 week and 3 days before the party arrived.

## Environmental Hazards

- **The Raindrop.** A single drop of rain acts like a falling rock trap.
- **The Surface Tension.** A puddle is an impassable barrier or a sticky trap (Athletics check to escape).
- **The Wind Gust.** A breeze can blow players off a leaf-platform.
- **Frozen leaves.** Must pick around fallen leaves frozen into the path.
- **Slippery mud / difficult terrain.**

## Gods & Religion (Early Concept)

Aesthetic direction: Egyptian-style gods with mundane forest animal heads on bugmanoid bodies (six limbs, thorax and abdomen). Mayan-ish zone aesthetic.

Mapping process: Akan god → Egyptian god but a whimsical forest creature → relevant D&D stat block if needed. Examples: humanoid with squirrel head, frog head, robin head, owl head — but the humanoid is a bugmanoid.

Open questions: Are ant queens minor gods? Gods are convenient for faction motivation — red ants could be worshipping or directly influenced by a local deity. Big swing: have the party kill a god, or make the BBEG a god. They probably shouldn't win in a straight fight — horcruxes, multi-component spell, or similar mechanic.

Very underdeveloped.
