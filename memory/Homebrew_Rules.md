# The Glade — Homebrew Rules & Mechanics

## Base Ruleset
D&D 5e (2024 edition), 99.9% vanilla with the modifications below.

---

## Character Creation

**Backgrounds:** Pick from background stat blocks/bonuses, not locked into flavor.

**Feat:** Players got one free feat when creating their characters

**Classes:** Vanilla D&D classes preferred. Banned subclasses:
- Twilight Cleric
- Peace Cleric
- Aberrant Mind Sorcerer
- Clockwork Soul Sorcerer

**Stats:** Standard PHB point buy, with one exception: you may drop one stat to 6 to gain two extra buy points.

**Starting level:** 3

---

## World Mechanics

### Flying
Basically a boosted jump. Not true flight.
- Height limited to 1.5× 5e normal
- Full movement laterally
- Cannot end turn in flight
- Follows normal movement rules otherwise
- Costs movement
- Provokes opportunity attacks when ascending (noted in one reference — confirm if this is locked in)

### Climbing
- Roll to climb per 5e but slightly easier
- Bugs without wings: out-of-combat climbing is without rolls

### Bugsmiths
Armor and weapons can be either:
- Traditional "hold/wear the thing"
- Bug anatomy modified by a bugsmith (e.g., sharpened mandibles, reinforced carapace)

Bug body parts can be sharpened and enchanted (armor, slicy arms).

This is all just flavorwashing standard D&D 5e weapons, enchantments, and magic items. 

### Currency: Life Pitch (LP)
- 1 LP = 1 HP
- Money is literally health potion in this world
- Currency and healing are the same resource — spending money costs potential healing
- Unclear how balanced this is, still hasn't come up. Will need to refine.

## Leveling

**Milestone-based.** Levels awarded in chunks tied to major story beats, not granular XP.

- Players gain 3 levels every time they cross a magical "bloom" border. So on Winter -> Spring (The Overgrowth) they hit level 6, then from The Overgrowth -> Summer (Name TBD) they hit level 9 and finally Summer -> The Fall they hit level 12. 

**Some flavor to synergize with homebrew mechanics:**
- The Bloom's magical shockwave has amplified all magic — bugs are getting more powerful the closer they get to the epicenter. This applies to all bugs including PCs.
- "What is X bug but MORE? What does a scorpion or even aphid with its magic cranked up to 11 do?" — this frame applies to monsters AND PCs.

## Bug-Scale Exploration

- A tree trunk takes 5 - 30 minutes to walk around
- A mouse pelt is a trophy-sized object
- A scorpion's stinger is a museum piece
- Weather events (rain, wind) can have an outsized affect

**Environmental hazards (concept list — not all in play yet):**
- **The Raindrop:** A single drop of rain acts like a falling rock trap
- **The Surface Tension:** A puddle is an impassable barrier or a "sticky" trap (Athletics check to escape)
- **The Wind Gust:** A breeze can blow players off a leaf-platform
- **Frozen leaves:** Must pick around leaves frozen into the path

**Bug-scale conversion:** Open question. One reference suggests 5 ft (game scale) = 1 inch (human scale). Not confirmed. Hasn't really been an issue yet.
