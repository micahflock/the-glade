# Session 6 (and beyond) Ideas

Forward-looking ideation pulled from the session 5 drive-home debrief. Intended as input for `/session-generate` later.

## Proposed broad structure for session 6

1. **Finish the Gleam conversation.** Gleam needs to give them a real next step toward the Leafcutter Queen — we have to actually hammer this out before the session.
2. **They take the room Gleam offers.** Likely outcome; if they don't, we improvise.
3. **The next morning, at breakfast, they meet the grieving bee** (or some other breadcrumb) and get pulled into the side quest to clear a mantis out of a flower patch.
4. **Combat encounter with the mantis.** This is the headline set piece for the session — see design notes below.
5. **More Canopia exploration.** Possibly the Royal Jelly Society; possibly a second swing at a heist.
6. **Option to stay another night in Canopia** so they can pursue whatever else they want to do in the city.
7. **End of session: they depart Canopia** for the next destination (open question — see below).

## Threads to develop

### Royal Jelly Society
- Keep the current plan: a kind of useless collection of bees obsessed with pressing flowers.
- Not plot-critical, but fun flavor. Worth offering as an optional Canopia stop.

### Flexing level 6
- The party just hit level 6 and we want them to feel it.
- The mantis-in-the-flower-patch side quest is the intended showcase.

### Getting to the Leafcutter Queen
- Gleam needs to give them a real handoff toward the Leafcutter Queen.
- We may **waylay them on the way** — they don't necessarily have to actually reach the Queen this session. The plan can get disrupted, but there should be a plan to disrupt.

### Heist at the Queen's Crown
- They engaged with the heist idea before getting bounced. They may want another shot.
- If they actually plan a heist, we want a real structure for it: clear plan in, meaningful obstacles, and a sizable payoff (monetary or otherwise) on success.
- They might also take pity on us as DMs and skip it. Either is fine.

## The mantis fight — design problem

The party is **3 squishy level 6 PCs with a lot of CC**. Looking for Claude's input on how to make this combat compelling. Constraints and worries:

### Why a single big mantis is scary
- If they land CC, they trivially wail on it.
- If they don't land CC, the mantis probably annihilates them.
- Feels too binary — no real middle ground, hard to balance, easy to TPK.

### Battlefield concerns
- We've established that the overgrowth can't be easily navigated by bugs on foot.
- How do they even reach the patch? How do they locate a single mantis once there?
- Movement options: Nancy can jump, Tabitha can jump, Drix has limited flight.
- One option: a map of distinct flowers as discrete platforms, with the party flitting between them.

### Encounter shape ideas
- **Mantis + spider crab henchmen.** Big bad plus minions gives the party more levers and matches their preference for fighting small groups of elites. Mantises are normally solitary, but we don't necessarily need a strong in-universe reason.
- **Stealth/ambush mantis** that fades in and out, repositioning between attacks.
- **Illusory duplicates.** The mantis is the orchid mantis we already statted (camouflages with flowers). Lean into that with magic from the source — illusory duplicates of itself give us more targets and more interesting decisions for the party.
- **Secondary objective.** Pure "kill the thing" is the binary failure mode. Give them another axis: an objective to protect, a weakness to exploit, or a tool to deploy.
- **Capture instead of kill.** Maybe they're given (or build) a tool that binds the mantis — Empire Strikes Back, Snowspeeder vs. AT-AT energy. A web to entrap it could be a fun planning beat going into the fight.

> Note: orchid mantis stat block already exists — don't regenerate it.

## Bigger open questions for the DMs

### What is the end goal of this arc?
- How does the Leafcutter Queen / Canopia thread push the party closer to the **source** (the magical upswelling)?
- It probably doesn't make sense in-universe for the party to just show up and kill the **Red Ant Queen** — but maybe we leave it on the table as an option.

### Where do they go after Canopia?
- Players seem to expect the next stop is the Red Ant / Leafcutter nest. That could work.
- We need to eventually push them toward the **summer zone** ("the scorched place"), which locks us into a fire theme.
- **Possible simplification:** base the Red Ant Queen in the scorched place, so the next destination collapses both threads into one.

### Sequencing
- DMs need to align on the actual next destination before session 6 wraps so the departure from Canopia points somewhere coherent.
