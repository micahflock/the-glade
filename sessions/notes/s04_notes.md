# Session 4 — DM & Player Notes
*Compiled from DM play notes and Heather's handwritten player notes (4/16/2026)*

## Observations Added by Players

- **Six leafcutters** were traveling with the party toward the Winter Farm.
- The leafcutters with the party are **young (~1 year old)**; Heather noted that elders are unhappy with the current state of affairs. This is a useful worldbuilding hook — age-based dissent within the colony.
- **Red ant bodies were still warm to the touch** long after death. This is a new, player-flagged detail about the fire magic — their body heat doesn't fade the way an insect's should. Connect to the magic gradient the rebels describe later.
- Heather noted the road itself was well-traveled and well-maintained — not a neglected or abandoned track.

## Creative Plays

- **Nancy's Message cantrip threat to Greg.** Before the ridge, Nancy sent Greg the Message: *"I'm watching you, and I will kill you if you act out."* Greg rolled a 6 on his save. Bought them until the descent.
- **"Poopheads"** was established as the code word for Nancy to Message Drix in-character. Consider recurring.
- **Drix's pre-emptive fungus cover story.** When Greg bolted across the river, Drix — still in fire-ant disguise — loudly accused Greg of "acting crazy, fungus in his brain." This arrived *before* Greg did and killed his credibility on entry. The two-guard contingency in the session notes never fired. The party got one red ant at the gate, which Drix bluffed past cleanly.
- **Nancy communing with the fungus.** Unplanned. Sensed magic in the mycelium, noted the Ahganmin was glowing more brightly than usual. Ate a small piece of fungus (no effect) and kept some for later study.
- **Drix's lullaby to the curtain.** The curtain relaxed; Drix cried. Beautiful character beat — the bard's first quiet moment.
- **Drix's Healing Word on a fungal wound.** After Nancy cut a piece off the curtain and it keened, Drix *healed* it. This calmed the alarm. Worth noting as a precedent — Healing Word appears to work on this kind of fungal tissue.
- **Tabby's direct question to the sympathetic ant:** "How did the last person get through?" Clean solve — the ant told her "soft, then stomp, then hush."

## Puzzle Flow — The Fungal Curtain

The curtain puzzle ran long and the party worked it as intended — fail-forward, multiple approaches, fail states consumed but no hard lock:

1. Mage Hand tickle → curtain shivered, no progress.
2. Drix's lullaby → curtain relaxed; Drix cried; Drix tried to walk through, released a cloud of shimmering spores, did not pass.
3. Nancy cut a piece → the curtain *keened* (matching the third-wrong-attempt "discipline response" design).
4. Drix's Healing Word → keening stopped.
5. The sympathetic ant advised them to let the curtain relax.
6. Nancy/Tabby/Drix started stomping — too aggressive.
7. Tabby asked how the last person got through.
8. Ant: **"Soft, then stomp, then hush."** Nailed it.
