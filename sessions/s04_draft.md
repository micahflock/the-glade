## 1. The Approach to the Winter Farm

### On the Road

As the party travels toward the farm with their leafcutter guides, the road deteriorates — muddy, then frozen, poor footing, rutted deep with wagon tracks all heading the same direction.

It's been getting colder. Snow falls.

**Rebel Guide's Knowledge Level:** The sympathetic leafcutter ant is NOT formally part of the rebellion. He's heard whispers, he knows the general area where the rebels operate, and he can get the party close — but he doesn't know the way in. "I've heard whispers. I know the area they're in. I can get you close, but they're very secretive." This prevents the guide from trivializing the search for the rebel cell once inside.

### Bestie — Decision Point

Before they reach the ridge, the party needs to deal with Bestie. She cannot proceed unnoticed into the Winter Farm.

**Options:**
- **Let her go before the farm:** She wanders away, never to be seen again. The party gets to take **one supply item** off of her before she leaves.
- **Bring her to the farm:** When the ants see her, they'll treat her as food. "Good find — that'll feed a lot of them." The dumb animal gets sent down to the farming chambers. The party won't be able to save her once she's inside.
- **Other:** Players may come up with something creative. Roll with it.

Give them the choice. Don't force it.

### Cresting the Ridge

They approach a moldering pine cone resting on the lip of a ridge — large enough to use as cover. Their leafcutter guide motions them low.

Read or adapt:

> From behind the pine cone, the ground drops away into a shallow valley hung with thick mist — it wasn't there a minute ago, but here, settled into the low ground, it's dense enough to muffle sound and swallow distance. You can't see the far side.
>
> A wagon trail zigzags down a rocky slope — not steep, but deliberate, the path shaped by engineering that doesn't care about straight lines or level ground. The dirt has been moved, packed, reshaped. Not fortifications — convenience. Repeated, functional, efficient. You wouldn't have seen any of this from the other side of the ridge, but from here, the ant earthworks are unmistakable: this place is used.
>
> At the bottom, a frozen stream crosses the valley floor, bridged by packed earthen ramps. On the far bank — barely visible through the mist — the creek-side wall has been opened. Three, maybe four entrances, each wide enough for a loaded wagon, built solidly into the hillside. Not exactly evenly spaced. Not decorative. Just there.

**Perception DC 14:** A faint, eerie glow from inside the entrances — barely perceptible, a dim luminescence that could be imagination.

A few leafcutter ants idle near the largest opening. They're looking up the road, expecting another wagon. No red ants outside. Eerily still in the mist.

**Note:** The body wagon the party intercepted was a straggler — late, and now it's never arriving. The farm entrance goes underground, beneath the zone barrier into the Overgrowth. This is a one-way-in situation.

## 3. Getting In — Player Options

**Play dead (expected/coolest option):** Disguise themselves as corpses being hauled in by the leafcutter guides. The guides approach the entrance: "Cart broke down. We're bringing them over one by one." The idle leafcutters head off toward the supposed broken cart, and the party moves in unimpeded. If the players don't think of this, have a leafcutter guide suggest it.

**At the gate:** A red ant is inside directing traffic. "You guys are late. Take that cart that way." If they follow directions, they'll be guided into the main corridors where it's busy and harder to break away unnoticed.

**Key layout:** Red ants patrol the **main corridors** and some side corridors. But the **farming chambers** are all leafcutters — red ants don't bother going down there. If the party can make it to a farming chamber, they can get out of the cart and move freely.

**Stealth:** The guides know patrol timing. Sneak past while the entrance ants are distracted or on rotation.

**Just walk up:** If they see only leafcutters and no red ants, they might just chance it. The idle leafcutters are not hostile — some may even be sympathetic.

**Distraction:** Create a diversion to pull the entrance ants away, then slip in.

## 4. Inside the Winter Farm

### Sneaking Through — Theater of the Mind

Don't run this as an elaborate stealth sequence. Once the party is past the entrance and heading toward the farming chambers, narrate the journey with atmosphere and one simple check.

> The ant leads you through twisting tunnels. The walls are packed firm — smooth, hard earth shaped by thousands of mandibles. The air warms as you go deeper. Trailing patches of bioluminescent fungus cling to the ceiling, casting everything in a dim, blue-green glow.
>
> Every sound carries: the scrape of chitin on dirt, the wet tearing of fungal threads, the low murmur of workers deeper in.
>
> His arm reaches out — he frantically waves you to stop.

**Stealth DC 8.** Everyone rolls. Only on an absolute catastrophic failure does a red ant hear something and come over to investigate — and even then, it just peers around a corner and moves on. Nothing happens. This is a feel-it moment, not a real threat.

Then continue:

> Your guide moves quickly. She doesn't explain the route.

### The Fungus Chambers

They pass many of these. Round rooms, different sizes. In each one: roughly spherical masses of insect bodies held together with pale fungus — pressed and twisted around each other the way leaves would be in a traditional fungal garden, except these aren't leaves. Some of the spheres are small. Some nearly fill their chambers. All of them are crawling with leafcutter worker ants carefully tending the growth.

The fungus has no reflection. It's dull, fleshy, twisted around the shapes underneath. In the dim bioluminescent light, the forms are hard to make out at first — and then suddenly, horribly clear. A wing. A curved shell. A mandible.

The workers ignore the party entirely. They have jobs. They do their jobs.

**High Perception / Investigation DC 14:** The temperature is rising slightly the deeper they go. The fungus on the larger spheres appears more mature — thicker, with visible fruiting bodies. The smaller, newer spheres still show recognizable anatomy underneath the growth.

### The Fresh Leaf Room

Then they pass a larger chamber — the biggest so far — and even in the dim fungal light, something flashes. A gleam of impossible color.

They look in. The ceiling is high here, patched with glowing fungus like a low sky full of pale stars. And in the center of the room, like a jewel set in the dark: a towering sculpture of perfect green leaves. Stacked, layered, arranged — every edge crisp, every surface bright and wet with sap.

It is striking. A flash of brilliance in the gloom.

Nothing about it is decaying. Nothing about it is even wilting. These leaves could have been cut this morning. They were not.

The guide pauses here, if only for a moment.

> "We cut those weeks ago. They should be mulch by now. They should be feeding the fungus. But nothing from the green side breaks down. It just stays."

She doesn't gesture toward the fungus chambers. She doesn't have to.

## 5. The Puzzle — The Fungal Curtain

**DM Note:** Both DMs should do another pass on this puzzle to make sure it's not too hard. The pheromone lock puzzle is being saved for later — potentially the combined ant nest deeper in the Overgrowth.

This is the hidden access point to Sporemother Vell's rebel cell, buried inside a legitimate processing chamber of the Winter Farm. The party can reach it while still under cover, but the scene turns tense because the correct answer is rooted in old leafcutter practice, not the harsh marching cadence the fire ants have imposed.

### What It Is

A curtain of pale fungus and threaded mycelium blocks a narrow service passage once used by leafcutter workers moving compost into the deeper beds. The passage is now the rebels' route. The fungal curtain is alive and vibration-sensitive. It opens only to the old harvest rhythm used to wake, feed, and calm the bed.

The fire ants do not know the old method. They think the route was sealed years ago.

### Read-Aloud: First Look

> The chamber beyond the cart route is warmer than the rest of the farm, damp with a heavy, sweet-sour rot. Tiered compost beds rise in shallow terraces, each furred over with pale fungus that glistens in the dim amber light. Along the far wall hangs what first looks like a curtain of white mold draped between root-pillars, thick as wool and wet as breath.
>
> It is not still.
>
> The hanging fungus gives a faint, collective tremor every time something heavy lands in the chamber. Thin cords of mycelium run from it into the floor, into the compost beds, and into three hollow root-ribs growing out from the wall around it like the frame of a buried harp. The stone beneath the curtain is worn smooth in an arc, as though generations of workers once stood there, then stopped.
>
> Somewhere overhead, deeper in the farm, you hear the barked rhythm of fire ant commands. The curtain does not stir for that at all.

### What Is True

- The correct pattern is: three soft taps, one heavy feed-strike, a full pause, then part the curtain slowly.
- The fungus responds to rhythm and stillness, not words, pheromones, or disguise.
- Fire ant marching cadence is the wrong answer and actively irritates the bed.
- The party does not need the exact pitch, only the right sequence and tempo.
- If the players describe the correct approach clearly, no roll is needed.
- If they are partly right, rushed, or improvising under pressure, use the execution DCs below.

### The Correct Sequence

- Three light taps on the hollow root-ribs or the floor just before the curtain.
- One solid thump at the center of the worn stone.
- Absolute stillness for one full breath cycle of the fungus.
- A slow, downward parting motion through the curtain with hands, tools, or mandibles.

A simple player declaration like, "We tap softly three times, stomp once, wait for it to swell, then pull the curtain down and apart," should solve it.

### Clues and DCs

Give clues freely. This works better as assembly than guessing.

- **Passive Perception 13:** The party notices the curtain shivers at nearby footsteps, but only settles when the chamber goes still.
- **Perception DC 12:** The three root-ribs around the curtain are worn smooth at hand height, while the center floor stone bears a single deeper dent from repeated impact.
- **Investigation DC 12:** The polished arc in front of the curtain suggests workers used to stand here deliberately, not just pass through.
- **Nature DC 13:** The fungus is reacting to vibration patterns. Loud repeated stomping agitates it. Gentle taps followed by stillness seem more promising.
- **Survival DC 13:** The curtain has a breathing cycle. It tightens, then loosens, in slow pulses. There is a clear moment when it is easiest to part.
- **Insight DC 12 on nearby leafcutters:** Even under fire ant command, some workers unconsciously move in a gentler old labor cadence when handling the compost beds.
- **History DC 14 or Nature DC 14:** This feels ritualized rather than mechanical, an old agricultural practice preserved in the body long after the words were lost.
- **Performance DC 13:** A character can test and mirror the chamber's rhythm without immediately tripping the harsher fail state, learning whether the bed wants softness, force, or silence.
- **Investigation DC 14:** A scratched worker-mark near the base of one root-rib shows a simple notation: three short lines, one deep mark, then an empty gap.

### The Sympathetic Ant

If you want an in-fiction nudge, place a tired leafcutter worker nearby sorting rot from non-rot. She is not an open rebel, just someone who has seen enough.

**How to Spot Her:**
- **Insight DC 12:** She flinches when fire ants pass, but her attention keeps drifting toward the curtain.
- **Perception DC 12:** She subtly stops another worker from thumping compost in the fire ant cadence.

**How to Get Something Useful:**
- **Persuasion DC 13** spoken quietly while cover is intact: She mutters a fragment without looking at them.
- **Deception DC 14** while still disguised or posing as labor detail: She gives the clue because she thinks they already belong here.
- **Intimidation DC 15:** She gives the clue, but this adds one Suspicion step immediately.

**Her Fragmentary Clue:**
> "Not the red beat. Soft first. Feed it once. Then hush."

If they press her for more, she shuts down. She does not know the final part clearly, only that the bed "listens when everyone stops."

### Execution DCs

Only call for these if the players have incomplete information, are improvising fast, or are attempting the solution while guards are close.

- **Performance DC 14:** Reproduce the rhythm cleanly.
- **Sleight of Hand DC 13:** Deliver the taps and parting motion with enough precision not to snag the living curtain.
- **Nature DC 14:** Time the pause correctly with the fungus's breathing.
- **Group Stealth DC 13:** Hold the chamber quiet during the stillness without someone shuffling, whispering, or clattering gear.

One strong success from a clever lead character can carry the scene if the description is good. If multiple characters want to contribute, let one make the main execution check and one other grant advantage by setting tempo, watching the breath cycle, or keeping lookout.

### Success Read-Aloud

> After the third soft tap, the hanging fungus gives a faint ripple. The heavy strike sends a tremor through the floor and up the root-ribs. Then, as the chamber stills, the whole curtain seems to inhale.
>
> The white strands loosen. Pale bulbs within the mat brighten one by one, not with firelight but with a cold, milk-blue glow. When you draw your hands downward through it, the fungus parts without resistance, peeling open in thick wet folds. Behind it lies a narrow tunnel, warm, dark, and very much in use.

### Fail States

Use fail-forward escalation, not a hard lock.

**First Wrong Attempt: Spore Cough**
- The curtain contracts and puffs a cloud of fine, sweet spores into the air.
- Constitution save DC 12.
- On a failure, the creature coughs or goes lightheaded, taking disadvantage on its next Stealth or Sleight of Hand check in this area.
- On a success, no further effect.
- The party now knows the curtain dislikes whatever rhythm they just used.

**Second Wrong Attempt: Choir Hum**
- The root-ribs answer with a low, resonant hum that travels through the floor.
- Nearby workers go briefly still.
- Group Stealth DC 13 or Deception DC 13 to play it off as routine labor noise.
- On a failure, a pair of leafcutter workers or one suspicious fire ant subordinate passes by to check the chamber. This does not automatically blow cover, but it adds pressure and limits time.

**Third Wrong Attempt: Discipline Response**
- The curtain shrieks, not loudly, but with a thin fungal keening that clearly means alarm to the colony.
- A discipline patrol is on the way in 1d4 rounds, or immediately if the party is already under suspicion.
- The secret route can still be opened afterward, but the party will have to do it while hiding, bluffing, or dealing with an interruption.
- If they flee, the chamber is now watched for the next few hours.

**Bad Wrong Answer: Fire or Repeated Hard Strikes**
If the party tries to burn through the curtain, hack it apart violently, or pound it with repeated fire-ant cadence:
- The curtain seals into a dense mat and releases a thick defensive spore cloud.
- Constitution save DC 13 or be poisoned until the end of the next scene.
- The rebel route is not destroyed permanently, but it becomes unusable for now.
- The party must find Vell another way, likely through prisoner pens, labor galleries, or an escorted route, which costs them secrecy.

### If the Party Gets Stuck

Use one of these after 1-2 failures or a lull:
- A sympathetic worker murmurs, "Too hard. It hates the red way."
- One compost bed pulses visibly in a slow inhale-exhale rhythm, making the pause obvious.
- Nancy or another sharp observer notices the curtain loosen only when the room falls completely still.
- Drix or another performer realizes this is a call-and-response rhythm, not a melody.
- Tabby or another tracker notices the worn arc and single heavy impact mark in the stone.

### Recommended DC Profile For This Party

This group is level 3 and reasonably strong on observation, improvisation, and skill coverage, so keep the puzzle readable:
- Easy clue DCs: 12
- Interpretive clue DCs: 13
- Full pattern confirmation: 14
- Hard social squeeze on the sympathetic ant: 15

## 6. The Rebel Cell Meeting

### The Meeting Room

Deep in the complex — past the working chambers, through a narrow passage partially blocked by a collapse that looks deliberate. A hollowed-out pocket behind a root mass. Low ceiling, walls reinforced with dried fungal slabs. A rough table made from a cross-section of twig. The bioluminescent patches here are thicker — deliberately cultivated. It smells like damp wood and secrecy.

**Note:** The rebel cell is on the **winter side** of the barrier. The party does not level up yet.

Vell and a few other leafcutters are waiting. They go very still when the party enters.

### The Cell Leader — Sporemother Vell

**DM Roleplay Assignment:** [DM 1] plays Vell.

**Species:** Leafcutter ant. Something above Worker but below Queen — a matriarch, a senior tender. Old enough that her generation remembers life before the Bloom. Some ants in the farm were born post-Bloom; she is not one of them.

**Title:** The ants in the rebel cell refer to her as the Sporemother — a title from her role as head fungus grower.

**Personality:** Fiery underneath a layer of steely composure. Maternal in the way of someone who raised an entire fungus garden for decades and watched it taken away. Her primary grievance isn't political — it's personal. She can't tend her leaf fungus gardens anymore. The old ways are gone. That's what drives her: not ideology, but the loss of the thing she was born to do and did beautifully.

**Voice:** Measured, deliberate. Every word placed carefully — she's still getting used to the idea that words can change things if you arrange them right.

**Key character note:** Vell has built this rebellion out of grievance, but even she knows there's no real future in it. Without an alternative to the alliance, the rebellion is just anger with nowhere to go. This is what makes the party's arrival so important — they represent the first actual hope.

**Roleplay note:** Leafcutters have always just done what their queen wants. Now their queen doesn't get to do what she wants. Rebellion is a totally new concept — they're inept at it. They've never had to organize laterally before. Vell is discovering what leadership means in real time.

### Vell's Introduction

"So. Someone from outside."

"They call me Vell."

She studies them.

> "I've been growing things my whole life. I know what fungus needs. I know what soil needs. I know what happens when you take the rot out of the world and leave everything perfect and green and completely, utterly useless."

> "The red queen took our queen. Took our purpose. Turned us into gravediggers. And my people just... did it. Because that's what we do. We're told, and we do."

Leaning forward:

> "I don't think it's supposed to work like this."

She should also convey that there's no future in the rebellion as it stands: "Even if we drive them out — how do we eat? What changes?" Plant this early. This is the core dilemma.

### The Nervous Doubter — Lieutenant

**DM Roleplay Assignment:** [DM 2] plays the nervous doubter.

**Species:** Leafcutter ant, older. Remembers the old ways too well. Terrified of what happens if the rebellion fails.

**Voice:** Anxious, theatrical worry. "But well! If we do nothing, we could let the fungus die! It could all be for naught!"

**Role in the scene:**
- Voices the counter-argument from inside the cell: the case for doing nothing, the fear of failure. Not a loyalist argument exactly — just the terrifying practical reality that if they stop, everyone starves.
- **Delivers key exposition about magic escalation:**
  - "The red ant queen has grown so powerful! She's wreathed in fire — how many of us must she incinerate?"
  - "And our queen — she's become unbalanced. Obsessed with her fungus. The way she threw herself into making a fungus that could eat bodies... it was disgusting." [MORE DETAIL: expand on what the leafcutter queen's enhanced abilities look like]
- This connects to Tabby's cold open observation — red ants shouldn't be able to cast fire. The magic gradient in the Overgrowth amplifies natural abilities.

### Other Cell Members

- **A true believer** — younger, possibly born post-Bloom. Doesn't remember the old ways but is furious on principle. Aggressive, maybe reckless. Comic relief potential, or does something impulsive that forces the party's hand.
- **A quiet competent** — doesn't talk much, handles logistics. Knows the tunnel layouts, guard rotations, supply schedules. The one Vell actually relies on.

### If the Players Reveal Their Quest

This is transformative for the rebellion. Right now the cell knows what's happening is wrong but has no plan for what comes after — and that's limiting recruitment. "Even if we overthrow them, how do we eat?" is the question that keeps fence-sitters loyal.

If the party tells Vell they're heading toward the source of the Bloom — toward a potential fix — this changes everything. It gives the rebellion actual purpose. A light at the end of the tunnel. This could bring critical new members in — the ones who've been held back by having no goal to work toward.

> "If what you're doing works — if things can rot again — we don't need the red ants. The alliance falls apart on its own."

### If the Players Don't Reveal Their Quest

Vell still asks for help. The rebellion needs it. She'll share more detail about the alliance, the raids, the scope of what's happening — and dangle a next objective to give the party direction.

### Vell's Pitch

> "We're not fighters. We're farmers. But we know every tunnel, every rotation, every chamber in this place. The red ants don't come down here. They think we're too stupid to be worth watching."

> "We want out. But if we just stop — if we refuse — everyone starves. Us. Them. The villages they haven't raided yet."

> "Even if we overthrow them... how do we eat?"

## 7. Information the Rebels Can Share

- **Alliance mechanics:** Red ants raid, leafcutters farm. Red ants get reliable, scalable food and leverage over the Overgrowth residents. Leafcutters get protection and survive. Neither side can function alone — the Overgrowth food doesn't decompose, and leafcutters can't raid.
- **Raid scope:** Ants raid outward from the edge of the Overgrowth. Either you ran or you got raided. Very few went toward the zone and came back — which is why no information has reached the outside world.
- **Dissent in the ranks:** Widespread disgust but no framework for resistance until recently. The concept of rebellion is genuinely new — leafcutters have always followed their queen.
- **Red ant power:** The red ant queen has become a very powerful magic user since the Bloom — the magic gradient amplifies natural abilities. Fire ants were always dangerous; now they throw fire.
- **Leafcutter queen:** Their queen's magic is amplified too — she's become extremely good at growing fungus. But she's under the red ant queen's control. She's not weak-minded — she chose the alliance because her colony would die otherwise. She doesn't have other options *yet*.
- **Red ant motivation:** Not just food — maintaining power and control over the Overgrowth residents. The fungal farms give them leverage.
- **Why the fire ant queen needs so much food:** She's planning on making lots of ants. She needs a lot of food. The leafcutters have always produced lots because they farm.

## 9. Vell's Mandate and the Tunnel

### The Mandate

Vell's directive to the party is **not** "free the queen." The leafcutter queen isn't a prisoner in the simple sense — she's rational, and she chose the alliance because her colony would die otherwise. She doesn't have options.

The mandate is: **bring this information to the leafcutter queen.** Tell her there may be a way to end the Bloom. If she knows there's an alternative, she can choose to break away from the fire ant alliance on her own terms.

Vell:

> "Our queen isn't foolish. She made the only choice she had. But if what you say is true — if there's a way to make things grow and rot again — she needs to know. That changes everything for her."

> "We have a path that heads that direction. Toward our homeland. They call it the Overgrowth now."

### The Tunnel

Vell or a cell member leads the party to an underground passage that runs from the Winter Farm beneath the zone barrier and exits on the Overgrowth side. No pheromone lock, no puzzle — just a tunnel. The rebels use it.

**As they walk,** the temperature shifts gradually. The packed earth walls change texture. The bioluminescent fungus thins out and then returns in different colors — warmer hues.

## 10. Passing Through the Barrier

They pass under the barrier, not through it. There is no visual — they're underground. Only the feeling.

> As you pass through an unseen threshold, the cold air of winter leaves your bug lungs (do bugs have lungs?) as you breathe out and see the fog in front of you dissipate. Your next breath is warm, and crisp, and humid. It's almost intoxicating after your long trek through the cold. You feel stronger, invigorated.
>
> No wait — you ARE stronger.

**The party gains 3 levels (Level 3 -> Level 6).**

Flavor for the level-up — adapt or read:

> Something that has been building inside of you, below your subconscious mind, has bloomed to the surface. With this last step, you feel suddenly more of yourself. More powerful. More assured.

**Design note:** The only thing they notice is the temperature change and the feeling. No visual barrier. No wall. No shimmer. This preserves the mystery of what the zone barriers actually are.

**End of Session 4.**

## DM Notes — Overgrowth Worldbuilding (Future Sessions)

These details came up in planning and should inform Sessions 5+. Not for Session 4.

- **Leafcutter infrastructure:** Leafcutters are critical in the Overgrowth because they keep pathways clear through the dense growth. This is a major part of the red ant-leafcutter alliance's soft power — they control the roads.
- **Red ant patrols:** The pathways also need to be patrolled. The magic gradient means predators are now imbued with wild magic and far more dangerous than before. Having red ant muscle defending the roads is compelling for Overgrowth residents.
- **Overgrowth residents don't know about the Winter Farm.** The body farming is hidden from the general population.
- **The Overgrowth is somewhat idyllic.** Nectar eaters, pollen feeders — bees are having a great time, butterflies are having a great time. Life goes on. The alliance is seen positively: "Oh great, they're keeping the roads open."
- **This drives the rebels underground.** When the bulk of the population is happy, the authoritarian regime's victims on the outskirts are invisible. The rebels can't get traction because most bugs don't see the problem.
- **Flying bug city:** Consider a city built into a tree, above ground. Flying bugs are starting to notice something is wrong — pathways that were open are now dense growth — but broadly things are fine.
- **Combined ant nest:** The red ant queen and leafcutter queen are in a combined nest at this point, not separate hills. This simplifies the confrontation.
- **Boss encounter — the Caterpillar:** One of the bosses in the Overgrowth zone is a caterpillar that is just too long. Unsettlingly long. It comes out of a hole in a tree and just keeps going. Magical properties TBD. Segmented mini needed — a flexible snake mini that can wiggle and cover multiple squares. [OPEN: full stat block and encounter design needed]
- **Wild magic predators:** Consider a wild animal encounter in the Overgrowth — something that shows how the magic gradient has warped formerly mundane creatures into something dangerous and strange.
