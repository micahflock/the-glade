# The Glade — Session Log

## Session 0 — The Beginning


The party assembled at the Chitinholm branch of the Royal Jelly Society, summoned by a general call to bugs of action. In a cramped upstairs room repurposed as a war room, they were briefed by the Sub-Committee on Impeding Winter Doom: Francesca "Boodle" Booloticia IV (merchant guild, jewel beetle), Dr. T'wigfingers (scientist, stickbug), and Sven (#7834, ant drone). The committee presented their theory: the unnatural cold gripping the Glade is not weather — it is a shockwave expanding from a single epicenter. Boodle's tip came from missing trade shipments; T'wigfingers had cross-referenced refugee data to establish directionality; Sven reported collapsed agricultural yields and raiding parties. The party was tasked with traveling to a lost RJS branch to retrieve records that, combined with Chitinholm's data, would triangulate a heading toward the source. In exchange: RJS membership, a title, and the legend. The session ended with the party moving through Chitinholm's market square.

## Session 1 — On the Road

Boodle made her exit immediately, leaving T'wigfingers to brief the party and hand off the sealed triangulation tube. After a pass through the RJS stockroom and stables — where Drix won over Bessie the sowbug with a walnut-shell lute performance — the party loaded up and headed east through Chitinholm. They passed through the packed market square without stopping, Nancy caught talk of dead farms near the gate, and Drix earned a coin or two busking on the road out. On the frozen road east, they ran into a family of shield bugs fleeing toward Chitinholm and then a cart under attack by earwig bandits. Drix called the bandits out first and took two rocks for it. The fight ended with Tabby's crits, Nancy's Sleep spell, Drix's Dissonant Whispers and Hold Person, and Nancy landing the killing blow on the leader — the rest scattered. The cart driver Bert ran past the party without a word, straight back to Chitinholm. The pillbug still hitched to the wagon didn't bolt — just watched. The party kept her.

## Session 2 — The Broken Wagon

The party repaired the damaged cart with a scavenged leaf for an axle, packed the bearing with grease, and pulled the broken wheel entirely — leaving a functional tri-wheeled rig. They swapped pack animals: Bessie was hitched to the repaired cart with a note from Drix tucked in the cargo, sent back toward Chitinholm on her own. Bestie, the pillbug from the wagon, came with the party. Camp was made between the root flares off the road; Tabby scouted, Nancy scoped the trail from high ground, Drix played lute and settled the site. The party finally opened T'wigfingers' sealed tube — data, not drama; a top-down map of Chitinholm annotated with lines and notations — and Tabby and Nancy began a physical reference map. Tabby took third watch and caught a faint glow to the east just before sunrise, which she marked on the map; by morning it was clear the glow was the village burning ahead of them. The next day brought the burnt village: no bodies, no food, only soot and Lars, the earwig who had run in Session 1, now fractured and barely present. He named two ant types — black and red — and pointed them northeast, where the bodies were dragged. The party left him with what he already had and moved on. Antennington was ransacked but standing; the RJS building yielded a note in green ink pinned with a hand-pickaxe, and Nancy found the Tune Hammer of Amber in the lab. At the Antennington Amber Mine, Ricko the ox beetle ran the lift, and Garett the green weevil waited on Level 2 with his triangulation parchment and a glowing magic amber rock — Ahganmin — which he pressed into Nancy's hands. Garett floated the possibility that the Bloom isn't a fluctuation of known magic at all but something new, and that Antennington sits at the edge of its effect. The party left the mine with three data sources now combined on their map and a heading toward the Bloom's source.

## Session 3 — The Body Cart

The party departed the Antennington Amber Mine after Nancy spent the night studying the Bloom with Garett, who built a detection box for the Ahganmin before they left. On the road east, fresh cart tracks in the mud confirmed ant traffic. The party went off-road and used Drix's Locate Animals or Plants to confirm ants ahead. Around a bend: a broken cart loaded with charred bodies, two fire ant commanders bullying four leafcutter workers into fixing a wheel. The party engaged — leafcutters fought against the party halfheartedly on the fire ants' orders, and as combat turned, some switched sides. All fire ants were killed, no leafcutters lost. In the aftermath, a loyalist leafcutter panicked about being implicated while a rebel declared the fire ants better dead, revealing the existence of a fungal farm growing food on corpses and a nascent resistance movement inside. The leafcutters also named "The Barrier" — a line across the land where nothing rots on one side and nothing grows on the other — and pointed out its location for the party to mark on their map, and framed the fire ants' grip as "protection" offered in exchange for following their direction. Nancy approached the loyalist directly — "We are on a mission to understand this bloom, might makes right doesn't sit with me" — leaving him cowed but unpersuaded, and promised the leafcutters the party would help protect them in exchange for cooperation. The session ended with the party planning to infiltrate the Winter Farm: play dead in the body cart, Drix disguised as a fire ant.

## Session 4 — The Winter Farm

The party traveled to the Winter Farm with their leafcutter guides as the road froze and snow began to fall. Nancy flagged the red ant bodies were still unnaturally warm long after death. Before the ridge, Nancy used Message to threaten the loyalist Greg — "I'm watching you and I will kill you if you act out" — and the party tied Bestie up with a snack and a blanket above the valley. From behind a pine cone on the ridge, they looked down into a misted valley with a frozen stream and three or four wagon-width entrances dug into the far bank. Drix cast Disguise Self to become a red ant; Nancy and Tabby used the disguise kit to play dead in the cart. On the descent Greg broke and bolted across the river, but Drix — in character as a red ant — pre-emptively yelled that Greg was "acting crazy, fungus in his brain." Greg vanished into the workers with his credibility destroyed. Drix bluffed past a single red ant at the gate and the party rolled inside. Deep in the leafcutter-only tunnels, Allen confirmed that the Sporemother did not like the fire ants; Drix dropped the disguise and Allen led them through fungus chambers and past the fresh-leaf room — "we cut those weeks ago, they should be mulch by now" — to a small processing chamber with a living fungal curtain. The puzzle ran long and beautiful: Mage Hand tickled, Drix's lullaby made the curtain relax and Drix cry, Nancy cut a piece (it keened), Drix cast Healing Word to quiet it, and finally Tabby asked the sympathetic leafcutter how the last person had passed through — soft, then stomp, then hush — and the curtain parted. Beyond it, Sporemother Vell and her rebel cell welcomed the party at a small round table. Vell confirmed the source of the Bloom, alluded to other species receiving amplified powers, and gave them her mandate: bring the news to the leafcutter queen so she can choose to break from the alliance on her own terms. The older cell assistant carved Runes of Fungal Protection into each PC — Nancy on the top-right pauldron, Drix as a "tramp stamp" on the lower back, Tabby on the back of the left shoulder. A leafcutter rebel named Antshley volunteered to escort them to the queen. The party left through a tunnel that descended under the barrier, warmth replacing cold without any visible threshold, and gained three levels (Level 3 → Level 6) as they stepped into the Overgrowth. Greg sucks. Allen rules.
