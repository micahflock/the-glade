# Session 4 — The Winter Farm
*Final session notes — reconciled with DM and player play notes*

---

## Cold Open

The party set off toward the Winter Farm with their leafcutter guides — six leafcutters in total, most of them young (~1 year old), with a subtle undercurrent of elder discontent about the current state of affairs. Tabby pulled Drix aside with a nagging thought: red ants are famous for caustic bites, not fire. Whatever they saw back there was beyond normal red ant behavior — something was wrong. Nancy noticed the red ant bodies from the road encounter were still warm to the touch long after they had died.

The road was well traveled and well maintained. The road deteriorated — muddy, then frozen, rutted with wagon tracks all pointing the same way. Snow began to fall.

---

## The Loyalist Problem

Before the ridge, Nancy sent the loyalist (**Greg**) a Message: *"I'm watching you, and I will kill you if you act out."* Greg rolled a 6 on his save. It didn't fully pacify him — but he held for now.

**Bestie decision:** The party tied Bestie up with a snack and a blanket and left her behind near the ridge. Not released, not brought in — parked.

---

## The Ridge

From behind a moldering pine cone on the lip of a ridge, the party looked down into a shallow valley hung with thick mist — a wagon trail zigzagging down rocky switchbacks, a frozen stream bridged by packed earthen ramps, and three or four wagon-width entrances cut into the far bank. A faint, eerie glow from inside the entrances, barely perceptible through the mist. A few leafcutters idled near the largest opening, watching up the road for a wagon that was never coming.

**Plan set:** Drix would Disguise Self as a red ant and drive the cart; Nancy and Tabby would use the disguise kit to play dead in the back alongside one of the actual fire ant corpses. **"Poopheads"** was established as the code word for Nancy to Message Drix.

---

## The Descent — Greg Bolts

As they worked down toward the frozen stream, heavy fog closed in around them. Glowing holes punctured the mist, and shapes of waiting ants resolved near the entrance. Sudden scuffling to the left — **Greg broke and ran** across the frozen river toward the farm.

**Drix's improv saved it.** In character as a red ant, Drix immediately yelled that Greg had been "acting crazy and has a fungus in his brain." Greg disappeared into the crowd of workers, his credibility pre-emptively destroyed. The two-guard contingency never fired.

A single red ant at the gate approached: "Why are you late?" Drix lied smoothly. The red ant believed him, waved the cart through, and the party proceeded into the Winter Farm.

---

## Inside the Farm

Mellow bioluminescent fungus lit the corridors, blue-green and close. Mostly leafcutters working; a few red ant supervisors near the main thoroughfare. The tunnels had been bored through the side of the riverbank into a series of interior passages. Nancy kept her bearings; the others were likely lost.

The tunnels narrowed until the cart could no longer pass. The party unloaded — still playing dead — and were carried down a side corridor. Drix followed in disguise. Soon the red ants fell away entirely; only leafcutter workers remained.

### The Fungus Chamber

The party was laid out beneath lacy fungus threaded with dead insect body parts — a leafcutter growing chamber. The workers tended them without suspicion.

The fungus was oddly beautiful up close. Nancy commune'd with it and sensed magic humming through the mycelium; the Ahganmin in Garett's detection box pulsed more brightly than they had seen before. Nancy tore off and ate a small piece of fungus — no ill effects — and pocketed some for later study.

### Allen Offers Help

After the cart was fully unloaded, **Allen** (the sympathetic leafcutter from the road encounter) approached Drix and quietly confirmed what they'd started to suspect: the Sporemother does not like the fire ants. Drix dropped the disguise and the tension broke. Allen asked directly whether it was true — a source of the Bloom, something the party could actually do about it.

He offered to take them to the Old Ones — leafcutters who remember the old fungus, who refuse to work for the Reds anymore. He led them through twisting tunnels used only by leafcutters, the air warming and growing heavy.

### The Fresh Leaf Room

They passed through a large, empty chamber with a striking centerpiece: a towering stack of emerald-green leaves, impossibly fresh, impossibly uncorrupted. No wilt. No decay. A flash of brilliance in the gloom. There was sorrow here — not decay — and no one said a word as they passed it.

---

## The Fungal Curtain

Allen led them to a smaller work room — a 15-foot chamber with exits on two sides, two workbenches of fungal bodies being refined for reproductive parts, and two pillars of fungus strung with a pale white curtain of mold between them. Here Allen paused: he didn't know how to go further from here. He walked away.

Nancy and Tabby scuttled closer. Tabby noticed the curtain subtly shivered as they approached. They stepped back, and Nancy tried tickling it with Mage Hand to see if he could tease a portal apart. It just shivered.

**Drix played a melodic lullaby.** The curtain relaxed. Drix felt it deeply enough to cry a little. He tried walking through — shimmering spores puffed into the air, and he was stopped.

**Nancy tried science.** He cut off a piece of the curtain. It *keened* — a thin fungal alarm-call. Drix cast Healing Word at the wound, and the keening stopped.

Tabby asked the sympathetic nearby leafcutter: how did the last person get through? The ant said they needed to let the curtain relax. She gave them the pattern:

> **"Soft, then stomp, then hush."**

They got it on the next try. The curtain inhaled, loosened, and parted around them — a warm, dark tunnel opening into the rebel cell.

---

## The Rebel Cell — Sporemother Vell

A narrow, dimly lit passage turned and opened into a chamber of brighter fungus and a small round table. Three leafcutters sat there — one clearly older than the others.

Nancy opened plainly: *"Y'all aren't happy about the red ants."* They parleyed; the rebels confirmed it. The old one named herself **Vell** — the **Sporemother**, head fungus grower, old enough to remember the time before the Bloom. She explained that her home is the Overgrowth, which the alliance has cut her off from. Nancy laid out the triangulation theory — the epicenter, the trough surrounding it — and confirmed that there is a source, and the party is heading for it. The party mentioned the scorched place as part of the picture they had assembled.

**Greg's name came up.** The group agreed, unanimously: Greg sucks, Allen rules.

### What the Rebels Shared

- Red ants started spitting fire when the Bloom started — Vell alluded to other powers given to other species.
- The red queen is subject to moodiness — volatile, dangerous.
- The leafcutter queen's situation is survival, not evil — she took the alliance because her colony would die otherwise.
- The rebellion has no future of its own. Without an alternative, it is just grievance with nowhere to go. The party's quest changes that.

### Vell's Mandate

Vell asked the party to bring their information to the leafcutter queen. Not to free her, not to overthrow her — to *tell* her. If the queen knows there is an alternative, she can choose to break from the alliance on her own terms. Vell can get the party there.

### The Rune of Fungal Protection

Before the party left, the older cell assistant (the nervous doubter of the cell) offered each of them a fungal Rune of Protection carved into their carapaces. "Some call it superstition, but us old-timers still believe in it. Let me give you what we give to all our best farmers."

- **Nancy:** top of the right pauldron / shoulder
- **Drix:** lower back (tramp stamp)
- **Tabby:** back of the left shoulder

The runes protect against fungal infection. *[Mechanical effect TBD — will matter later.]*

### Antshley

A leafcutter ant named **Antshley** volunteered to escort the party to the leafcutter queen. Heather clearly took a shine to her.

---

## Through the Tunnel — Into the Overgrowth

The party left the way they came, then Antshley led them down through a series of tunnels. The temperature rose. The walls smoothed. The fungus swirled into warmer hues.

They passed through an unseen threshold — no visual, no shimmer, no wall — and the cold air of winter simply left their lungs. The next breath was warm and humid and intoxicating. Something that had been building under their skin for weeks finally bloomed to the surface.

**The party gained 3 levels (Level 3 → Level 6).**

**End of Session 4.**
