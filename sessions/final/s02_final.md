# Session 2 — The Broken Wagon
*Final session notes — reconciled with DM play notes*

## The Battlefield — Damage and Supplies

As Bert's scuttling progress towards Chitenholm abruptly fades, silence descends over the battlefield. The earwigs lie where they fell, as motionless as the frost-locked world around them. Nothing stirs.

**If the players investigate the dead:**
Up close, they look more like farmers than bandits. Their clothes are well worn — lovingly patched at the knees and elbows, stitched and restitched until the original fabric is more memory than material. The most valuable things they have on them are their improvised weapons: a sawed-off scythe, a hoe on both ends.

The earwigs were not gentle with the cargo. The woven grass sides are torn in long ragged strips, and one rear wheel lists at a wrong angle. The supplies inside have shifted and scattered. At the front, the pillbug hitched to the harness shifts its weight as you approach, feet finding no comfortable arrangement on the frozen road. It doesn't bolt. It just watches you, with the particular patience of something that has learned not to expect much.

- **Investigation DC 10:** the axle beneath it is bent, maybe cracked
- **Investigation DC 15:** one front wheel bearing is also damaged
- **Animal Handling DC 14:** the pillbug is soothed

**What the party did:** Nancy found a straight piece of leaf suitable as a replacement axle. They packed the bearing with grease, then pulled the damaged wheel entirely. The cart became a tri-wheeled rig — underloaded but functional. Drix tucked a note into the cargo: *"Wanted to make sure that these supplies made it back to the city, bandits :(" — Drix.*

**The pillbug:** Still hitched to the wagon after the fight. Will wander home (not toward Chitinholm) if left unattended. The party named her Bestie.

**Bessie/Bestie swap:** The party elected to leave Bessie (the RJS sowbug) hitched to the repaired cart, sending the supplies and cart back toward Chitinholm on their own. They kept Bestie as their pack animal going forward. The cart — with Drix's note, Bessie, and the supplies — was set off down the road.

## First Night

**Choosing a campsite:**
The road levels off where a toppled birch log has come to rest against the base of one of the great firs. The light is rapidly waning. Three spots present themselves.

**Option 1 — Hollow under the log:**
The log came down at an angle, and where it meets the ground it's left a long low gap — dark, out of the wind, smelling something that lived here once and probably doesn't anymore. The interior is cramped but dry. Whatever made its home here left behind a loose scatter of chewed fiber and seed husks. No sightline out, and no sightline in.

**Option 2 — Between the tree roots:** *(chosen)*
Two roots diverge from the base of the nearest fir in a wide V, leaving a patch of cleared ground between them — sheltered on two sides, open on the others. The frost here is thinner than on the road, beaten back slightly by whatever warmth still seeps from deep in the wood. There's an old fire ring at the center. The stones are blackened, and old enough that you can't place who left them. Someone thought this was a good spot too, once.

**Option 3 — On top of the rock:**
A broad flat stone juts from the hillside above the road — granite, pale grey, swept clean by wind. From the top you can see the road in both directions, and the canopy opens just enough overhead that you'd have sky. It offers nothing in the way of cover or warmth. What it offers is visibility.

**Camp activities:**
- Tabby gathered firewood and scouted the perimeter.
- Nancy climbed to the crest of the root flare to scope the zone — could see the trail they'd been following.
- Drix set up camp: cleared sticks and rocks, spruced the site, played the walnut shell lute.
- Bestie was tied up for the night.

**The night — watch and unease:**
The scentless forest whispers into the night. The cold ticks in the branches overhead. Somewhere distant, something drops from a high limb into the snow. The fire holds.

- **DC 14 (watch only):** The pillbug has settled closer to the camp than it was at dusk. Not close enough to mean anything, probably. Just closer.

## Opening the Tube

The party opened T'wigfingers' sealed triangulation tube. They were expecting something exciting. They got data. Tabby and Nancy spread it out and started a map — marking the direction of the glow from T'wigfingers' calculations.

## The Road to Antennington — Frozen Fields

The party set off in the direction of Antennington. Farms begin to appear — the fields to either side of the road have been waiting nine months for a thaw that hasn't come. Whatever was harvested is gone. Whatever wasn't stands where it died: stalks and husks locked upright in the frozen earth, grey and desiccated, neither rotting nor living. The silence is only broken by the scratching of claws against the hard ground.

**First sign of trouble — the smell:**
Before you see anything, you smell it. Something burnt. Not just woodsmoke — something sharper, heavier, the kind of smell that stays in your antennae long after the source is gone.
- **DC 12:** It's the smell of a burned village.
- **DC 16:** There is a distinct tang of magical energy.

**Approaching the outbuildings:**
The first outbuilding looks intact from a distance. As you pass it, you see the doors are gone — not broken, not rotted. Wrenched off. The hinges are bent outward.

## The Burnt Village

The town square was built around the road, the way humble places are — everything oriented toward travelers, toward commerce, toward the hope of both. The homes that line it were once handsome. Fir-needle roofing with acorn cap shingles. Some are just foundations, chunks scattered and scorched. Some are half a blackened wall leaning against a collapsed roof. Others simply have a rough crater in place of their door, gaping at you.

No food. No bodies. Only soot, broken furniture, and random knickknacks and keepsakes.

**Lars — south side of town:**
*DC 8 passive perception:* You hear it before you see him: a low, broken chittering from the south side of the square.

He's crouched against what's left of a wall, facing directly at you. It takes a moment to place — the earwig. The one that ran.

He's not looking at anything. When he finally speaks, the words come out in pieces, like he's struggling to recall a dream.

*"The raiders came. We should have been there. They're all dead."*

A pause. Then, quieter, almost to himself:

*"The ants. The ants. They took them all."*

**What the party learned:** Two types of ants — black and red. Bodies were dragged in a northeastish direction. The party marked this on their map.

**Lars's fate:** The party left Lars where they found him, with the supplies he already had from the raid. He made no move to follow. The party moved on.

## Antennington

To call Antennington a town was very generous. An outpost was closer to the truth — its status as a town owed entirely to the now past-its-prime Amber Mine at the outskirts. In generations past, much wealth and magical energy was pulled from the very earth beneath this place. The riches did not stay with the inhabitants. A few clustered homes, a town hall, a couple of sparse market stalls, and a small RJS building. That is all.

As the party moves through town, unease grows. Deadly quiet. No sounds of merriment or disagreement. Doors are broken down or thrown wide open.

**Attack timing:** Antennington was hit approximately 1 week and 3 days ago.

**Ransacked houses:** Cabinets opened, drawers pulled out. Nothing of use left behind.

---

## The RJS Building

The RJS was not spared. Its ornate wooden door is wide open, held aloft by only one remaining hinge. The interior is especially ransacked — many cabinets and drawers turned over.

The building is laid out like a small school: a small entry area, an adjoining hallway, three rooms off the hall.

**Room 1 — Meeting Room:**
Mostly intact. Round table with chairs. One cabinet opened, contents dumped on the table: mostly documents about the mine and old expeditions.

**Room 2 — Lab:**
What must have once been a working lab is nothing but a mess. Cabinets and boxes of delicate instruments and tools turned out and strewn across the room.
- *DC 12 Perception/History:* The equipment was mostly for assessing the value of material coming out of the mine.
- **Of note:** Pinned to the wall with a small hand-pickaxe, written in green ink: *"ALL RJS MEMBERS MEET IN THE MINES FOR EMERGENCY MEETING — G"*
- **Tune Hammer of Amber** (DC 14 Investigation): A small ball-peen hammer. When struck against amber, it produces a note — the pitch changes depending on the age of the amber. **Nancy found and kept it.**

**Room 3 — Breakroom / Staff Only:**
The most thoroughly picked over. Oddly neater than the others — the ransackers apparently had to clean up after themselves as they worked.

## The Antennington Amber Mine

The Antennington Amber Mine (AAM) was once the pride of the town. As the amber ran thinner and demand lessened, it became less lucrative. Now it is mostly maintained as a remnant of history — a motion the town keeps going through because it doesn't know what else to do. A relatively shallow pit mine.

The main building is easy enough to find. A pickaxe-shaped knocker on the front door.

**Ricko — Surface:**
A deep voice from within: *"Hello? The door is open, come on in."*

The building interior is one room. Easy to find the massive Ox Beetle working on a piece of amber.

*"Hello! Must say, I was not expecting ANYONE to come through here anytime soon. Either I am very lucky for your company. Or you are with the RJS? I'm Ricko!"*

He didn't see the attack on Antennington — was in the mines when it happened. He knows Garett is on Level 2.

*"Garett is down on level 2 of the mine. He says he needs the isolation of the mines for his instruments to get their best measurements. I can't say I understood all the words he used. But we stopped mining on level 2 years ago, so he's not in anyone's way! Would you like to take the lift down?"*

Ricko opens the lift gates and gestures the party inside. The lift rumbles menacingly but safely down to Level 2.

**Garett — Level 2:**
Directly outside the lift on the Level 2 landing: a mildly lit table. A Green Weevil fussily hunched over it. He turns at the noise of arrival.

*"Oh! You aren't Ricko! Now this is a surprise. Who could you be, what do you want?"*

The lift heads back up behind the party as they talk.

*"Yes, I was at the RJS when the ants moved in to town. I heard them hit the first home. I peeked out to see what the noise was. When I saw what was happening, I decided that the mine was probably a safer place. I have never seen an aggressive ant before, and I can't say I want to meet one."*

*"If you've come for my triangulation data, I have it here."* He holds out a rolled-up piece of parchment.

*"While I am capable of getting the data, it's not really within my wheelhouse to use it. So it is here I shall stay. I will not be joining you in triangulating or finding the source of this event that I have been calling 'The Bloom.' I am much more interested in the magical element of the event. Alongside the chill, my instruments picked up a slight fluctuation in the magical flow in this area. Fortunately, I had set up in the mine already — my instruments would never have been able to pick up the minute changes if we were in the RJS building back in Antennington."*

**What Garett gave the party:**
- Rolled-up parchment with triangulation data
- Named the event "The Bloom"
- Confirmed the magical shockwave component alongside the cold
- **Ahganmin** — a chunk of amber that glows and reacts to elevated levels of magical energy. *Nancy is holding it.*
