# Session 3 — The Body Cart
*Final session notes — reconciled with DM play notes*

---

## Departure from the Mine

Nancy spent the night at the Antennington Amber Mine in extended conversation with Garett about the Bloom's magical properties. Before the party departed, Garett built a small detection box for the Ahganmin — a housing that makes changes in the amber's glow easier to observe.

The party made breakfast, hitched Bestie, and set off east along the road toward the heading their combined triangulation data pointed to.

---

## The Road — Cart Tracks

The forest passes by uneventfully, until the party reaches a slight incline in the road leading to a patch of mucky, muddy ground. Unpleasant on many accounts. But one thing it provides is a set of cart tracks.

- **DC 12:** The tracks are recent.
- **DC 16:** A few hours old at most.

The cart tracks continue in the direction the party is heading. Lots of sets — at least 6, hard to tell in the mud.

- **DC 16 (tracking):** The tracks are ant-sized.

**What the party did:** They discovered the tracks and determined the number of bugs. Rather than follow the road directly, they moved off-road — roughly 60 feet into the forest, paralleling the path from cover.

---

## The Encounter

As the party continued through the tree line, they began to hear voices ahead — a group of bugs loudly conversing around a few bends in the path.

Drix cast Locate Animals or Plants (2nd-level) to confirm: ants, and they were close.

**Overheard:**

Fire Ant 1: *"Get that wheel fixed! We need to get these bodies into the dark quickly!"*

Fire Ant 2: *"You heard the Bug! GET MOVING!"*

**The scene:**

A large cart propped up on sticks with one wheel partially off. Two leafcutter ants working to remove it — multiple broken spokes protruding. A spare wheel lies on the ground ready. Two fire ants stand at the head of the cart with another two leafcutter ants. The fire ants' gesturing and posture indicate displeasure with whatever the leafcutters are doing.

The cart is filled with the cracked and charred remains of bugs in various states of damage. Some charred, some crushed, others too damaged to identify.

**Leafcutter side conversation:**

Leafcutter 1: *"Can't believe what we have to do these days. We carry leaves, not CORPSES."*

Leafcutter 2: *"Quiet! Don't let them hear you! You know how hotheaded Fireys are."*

Fire Ant 1: *"Are you 2 done yet? You better not be stopping for a CHAT!"*

The leafcutters huff and redouble their efforts.

---

## Combat

The party engaged. The fire ants led the charge. The leafcutter ants joined the fight on the fire ants' side but halfheartedly — pulling punches, engaging reluctantly. As combat turned against the fire ants, some of the leafcutters switched sides and fought alongside the party. All fire ants were killed. No leafcutters were hurt.

---

## Post-Fight — The Loyalist and the Rebel

With the fire ants dead, the surviving leafcutters split into two reactions.

**The Loyalist:**

The moment the last red ant drops, one of the leafcutters physically recoils. He backs away from the bodies, mandibles clicking fast.

*"They're dead. Oh — oh no. They're dead. They're going to think we did this. Do you understand? They're going to come looking, and they're going to think we killed them, and then — what are we going to do?"*

He's not angry at the party. He's terrified — and now personally implicated, having been present when the fire ants died and having fought alongside them before switching. His framework: follow the queen, do what you're told, survive.

**The Rebel:**

One of the other leafcutters hasn't moved. She's been looking at the dead red ants. When she speaks, her voice is flat and sure.

*"Good. They're better dead."*

The Loyalist spins: *"Are you insane?"*

*"Listen to yourself. You know what we're doing. Hauling bodies. Growing fungus on corpses. This is a corruption. This is sickening."*

The Loyalist, urgent: *"They protect us. Remember what happened when we raided alone? Remember how many of us died? The red ants get us food. They get us protection. It's the only way to survive."*

The Rebel: *"The red ant queen dominates our queen. She changed us. She's corrupted the old ways. I'd choose death over this."*

A beat. Then, quieter, steadier: *"No — not death. I choose hope. That this isn't permanent. That something can be done."*

**Information revealed through the argument:**
- The leafcutters are farming fungus on decomposing bodies
- Red ants raid, leafcutters farm — neither side can function alone
- The leafcutter queen was coerced or dominated by the red ant queen
- There is a group organizing resistance inside the farm — the Rebel has a contact
- The leafcutters tried raiding alone before the alliance and it went badly (this was the Antennington raid)
- The Loyalist won't volunteer what they're farming; the Rebel will

**Nancy's move:** Nancy approached the loyalist directly — *"We are on a mission to understand this bloom, might makes right doesn't sit with me."* The loyalist was cowed but not won over. He will stay silent and betray the party at the earliest opportunity if not dealt with.

**The Rebel's reaction:** Impressed by the idea that the Bloom can be undone. This provides real hope for the rebel cause — a reason to resist beyond principle.
