## The Departure

Boodle clears her throat.

> "Well, I must be leaving, I have people to see, places to be. I leave you in the many capable hands of T'wigfingers."

Boodle bows and walks out of the cramped room.

T'wigfingers looks at the gathered party, sighs, then begins.

> "While some of our society are leaving, I believe our success lies in science and numbers. Have you heard of the act of triangulation?"

He looks around for answers, then continues regardless.

> "By taking measurements of when we started seeing this unusual winter, we have an estimated direction it came from. But we need more data to be sure. So we think you should head towards the RJS branch in Antennington. We sent a missive to them a week or so ago to begin gathering this data too. With their data and this data—"

T'wigfingers steps away from the table and shuffles papers on a desk behind him. He returns with an ornate tube — the kind used to transport important maps or documents.

> "In here is a timetable, data points, a map, and estimates of direction that the winter snap came from. I advise you to leave the tube sealed until you get to Antennington — that will prevent dirt and grime from getting into the container."

He slides it across the table.

> "Well, shall we get to some of the brass tacks? Questions? Any expedition starting out from a branch of the RJS has ample supplies at their fingertips — follow me to the stockroom."

## The Stockroom

T'wigfingers takes the party through the main stairwell and down a large hallway, stopping before a heavy door. He fishes a key from his coat pocket and unlocks it.

> *As T'wigfingers shows you through the stockroom, your eyes scan the room and have trouble settling on one thing. Rows of shelves, hooks, and stacks cover every surface.*

> "As every expedition leaving from here needs to be prepared, we keep our supply room well stocked! Feel free to take whatever you think you might need. I can't tell you what you will face on your journey. Also, I think we have an extra sowbug in the stables that you are welcome to take — the extra sets of legs could prove useful for more supplies!"

## Bessie

With the party laden with supplies, T'wigfingers escorts them down a back stairwell to a small paddock, stalls, and well.

> "Well, the only one left is Bessie."

He gestures at a small speckled sowbug in one of the stalls.

> "She's all yours! If you do get lost, just set her free — I imagine she will make her way back here eventually."

He shows them the tack room, then points toward the gates.

> "This is where I will leave you, I suppose! Any final questions? If you take the main road out of town and head east you will head directly towards Antennington."

He looked briefly at each of them, gave a quick nod, and retreated back into the safety of the RJS.

*Drix performed on his walnut shell lute for Bessie. She seemed to enjoy it.*

## Chitinholm Market Square

The RJS sits at the center of Chitinholm — a short block to the main road. The first section is residential: low acorn roofs, stone and spittle construction, small walled gardens. The residential section thins as the market square opens up.

> *The flow of others increases here, and there is a hum of activity in the air. The market is packed — Chitinholm must have doubled its population in the past few months. Small stalls clustered around hawk anything and everything. The market itself seems built around an ornately fuming chimney powering the baker and the bugsmith's forge. The comforting aroma of fresh nectar cakes mingles with the sweet scent of heated iron.*

**Stalls present (not visited this session):**
- Rhonda — Bugsmith (Goliath beetle)
- Hickum — Bakery (Carpenter bee): nectar cakes, pollen loaves, honey buns
- Bigsby's Buys! — Trinkets

*Nancy listened for news near the gate. She heard talk of dead farms and desperate refugees. The party skipped the market stalls. Drix played a song on the road out and the party earned a little coin.*

## Leaving Chitinholm

> *The scale and urgency of the disaster becomes obvious as you set off. The wide road leading away from the gate is packed with throngs of displaced bugs from every strata of society. Weevils with polished carapaces rub abdomens with sawflies. Sowbugs, wood lice, and pillbugs pull carts — some in teams — all heavily laden. Millipedes, saddle bags bulging, carry entire families.*

> *You're the only ones headed away from the city. You weave through the confusion, descending the root flare of the massive fir that Chitinholm is built around. Glancing back, the jagged spire of the Royal Jelly Society is just visible over the crowd.*

> *When you reach the forest floor, you are at the convergence of several wide roads cut through the frozen leaf litter.*

**(History check — Drix):** Jiminy Hendrix has performed in Antennington before and knows the route.

**(Perception DC 14 — group):** The forest is nearly scentless. The ground has been frozen long enough that even the gentle decay of leaf litter is absent.

> *The bustling road slowly dwindles to a rutted country path — two carts could not pass each other, though the whole party can walk abreast with room to spare. Occasionally they pick their way around fallen leaves frozen into the path before they could be removed.*

## Encounter — The Road Bandits

**A family of shield bugs** clatters around the corner ahead, moving as fast as their luggage allows. They eye the party uncertainly, not slowing, pushing toward Chitinholm. They pass without incident.

**A covered wagon** rounds the corner next, pulled by an obviously exhausted pillbug. A young cicada — **Bert** — urges it faster, glancing back with worry.

Behind him: a cluster of earwigs wielding farm implements and improvised weapons.

> *(DC 5: their clothes are ragged — no more so than other refugees. DC 10: they move with purpose — they aren't afraid of the cart. DC 15: they look vital and well-fed.)*

**Drix called out the bandits first and took two thrown rocks for it.**

The combat:
- **Tabby** opened with a crit dagger stab on one of the bandits.
- **Nancy** cast *Sleep* — 23 HP worth of creatures dropped. One earwig fell unconscious.
- **Drix** cast *Dissonant Whispers* — the leader was forced to flee, then returned.
- **Tabby** applied *Hunter's Mark* and continued working the leader.
- **Drix** cast *Hold Person* on the leader.
- **Nancy** killed the leader. The last pillaging earwig ran instantly.
- The remaining bandits scattered.

**Earwig backstory (if negotiated before combat):** Farmers whose village was destroyed by red ants — they turned to raiding to survive. Clothes lovingly patched, stitched until the original fabric is more memory than material.

**Bert** ran past the group after the fight — didn't stop, kept going to Chitinholm with one of his packs.

**The pillbug** — still hitched to the wagon. Didn't bolt. Just watched the party with the particular patience of something that has learned not to expect much. The party kept her. **(Bestie.)**

The party repaired the cart, attached Bessie to it, and sent it back toward Chitinholm with a note from Drix.
