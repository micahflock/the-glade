THE SCENE (Flavor Text)

This dusty room in the Chitenhom Branch of the Royal Jelly Society is packed with curiosities beyond anything you’ve seen before. Trophies of past expeditions crowd every available surface - preserved stamen, a massive scorpion's stinger, what must be nearly a full mouse pelt. Ancient and exotic weapons, delicate mechanisms encased in glass, even a preserved wasp larvae all compete for space on bookshelves loaded with dense tomes. 

This room has recently been repurposed. The large table you sit around must have been nearly impossible to squeeze up the narrow stairwell. Overstuffed chairs have been unceremoniously pushed against walls and bookcases, and a messy map has been hastily tacked to the table with no regard for the rich finish of the wood underneath. 

Three bugs sit across from you, compound eyes gleaming intently. One of them, an obsidian black ant, led you here with a furtive excitement, dodging the disapproving looks of other scholars as they ferried you through the labyrinth that is the Chittenholm branch of the Royal Jelly Society. 

A jewel beetle, shell polished to a shimmer, speaks first. The practiced warmth of her voice can’t quite cover the anxious undercurrent to what she says.

“Welcome, and thank you for coming .so quickly. Your individual reputations precede you... I am Francesca Booloticia IV. You can call me Boodle - I represent the interests of the merchant guilds here in Chitenholm. We are in dire need of bugs of action like you.”

The other two bugs give a start, as if shocked by the directness of Boodle's words. They fix their faceted gazes on you again, somehow even more intently than before. 

A nervous looking stickbug breaks an uneasy silence.


“I am Dr. T’wigfingers. I’ve been looking at our records captured around the beginning of this... event. We all remember - it was as if a gust of wind from the depths of winter cut straight through our balmy midsummer day. Travelers began to trickle in with tails of snowfall smothering new summer growth and wild raiders devastating towns. That trickle grew to a stream, then flood, of refugees from the east.”

Boodle breaks in:
“Of course, we were prepared. Chitenholm prides itself in its wealth, shared freely with those in need. An early winter might mean dipping a little deeper into our stores of Sap than we ever have before, but surely it wouldn’t be too long until the first shoots of spring began to break through the frosty ground?”

Dr. T’wigfingers again:
“As you must know by now, that spring has not yet come. Weeks late, at this point. I - we - suspect the worst. Most of the Royal Jelly Society is focused on Chitenholm: rationing, and how to prepare our population to evacuate our beloved home for the promise of warmer lands to the west. All without causing a general panic, of course”

The ant cocks their head at you “A source”. This is the most eloquent drone you’ve ever encountered. 

Boodle’s antennae wiggle excitedly. “Yes, Sven - Dr, T’wigfingers, you mentioned the records. You found a pattern to support the rumours I’ve heard.”

The stickbug takes a moment to compose himself. “We have a… Theory. Not a particularly popular one, but if it’s true... I cross-referenced the arrival dates of messengers bearing tidings of this great chill. Compared to when we lost contact with various towns, and timing of refugee asylum requests - it all points to one thing. An epicenter. Not a great wind, but a shockwave of cold expanding from a central point.”

Boodle “That’s where you come in. Bugs of action. If there’s even a chance we can figure out what’s going on, and somehow stop it…”
